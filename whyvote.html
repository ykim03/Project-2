WHY VOTE?

ISSUES YOU MUST KNOW BEFORE YOU VOTE

National Issues

MEDICARE

Medicare reform has Governor Romney and President Obama both agreeing on one thing- the need for change. Both candidates plan to transform the government-funded program that currently provides over 47 million older Americans with health care insurance. The difference lies in how much (and how quickly) each contender proposes to alter things.

Leading Proposals:

  • Increase the age of eligibility for Medicare to 67, from 65
  • Charge co-payments for home health care services and laboratory tests
  • Require drug companies to provide additional discounts, or rebates, to Medicare brand-name drugs purchased by low-income beneficiaries
SOCIAL SECURITY

Most seniors-aka Grannies-survive and heavily rely on Social Security. The Social Security system provides a core retirement income and it has lifted tens of millions of retirees out of poverty.

Baltimore Local Issues

GAMING EXPANSION(Q7)

Many grannies enjoy spending their valuable time with various activities, including playing games.Are you a gamer granny? Do you favor the expansion of commercial gaming in the State of Maryland for the primary purpose of raising revenue for education?

RECREATION AND PARKS AND ENVIRONMENTAL IMPROVEMENT LOAN

Recreation and Parks and Environmental Improvement Loan will provide funds for projects such as neighborhood recreation facility expansion and modernization, park rehabilitation, and planting street trees. Grannies could certainly spend their sunny days watching puppies and enjoying the freshly improved parks.

WALTERS ART MUSEUM LOAN

Walters Art Museum Loan will contribute to comprehensive renovation of the City-owned Walters' campus. Walters Art Museum is a free art museum open to publid, attracting great number of grannies in Baltimore. This popular gathering spot will be enhanced and renovated to provide better facility and environment for grannies looking for an extra spark for the day.
